Working as a private investigator, Jessica Jones comes to the aid of those who need it, despite suffering severe trauma at the hands of the sadistic Kilgrave.

“Marvel’s Jessica Jones” stars Krysten Ritter as Jessica Jones, with Rachael Taylor as Trish Walker, Carrie-Anne Moss as Hogarth, and Eka Darville as Malcolm Ducasse. The series is executive produced by showrunner Melissa Rosenberg along with Marvel’s Jeph Loeb.

Jessica's Powers

After being adopted, Jones discovered she possessed superhuman abilities due to the fatal car crash that took the lives of her family. However, a fateful encounter with Kilgrave, a vicious man with mind controlling powers, derailed her superhero career and she spent a torturous tenure as Kilgrave’s sex slave before finally breaking free of his control.

New York, New York

Jessica Jones is inseparable from New York, a city that mirrors her toughness and inner struggles. Hell’s Kitchen, with its restless energy, dark corners, and neon lights, isn’t just her backdrop but a reflection of her own resilience. The city shapes her, challenges her, and becomes as much a character in her story as Jessica herself.
